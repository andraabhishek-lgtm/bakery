Where It All Began

A Passion Project That Became Our Life

"I never planned to open a bakery. I just couldn't stop baking — and one morning, the neighbours started knocking."

What started as Sophie Duval's obsession with sourdough in a cramped Notting Hill flat became something much larger. After training under legendary Parisian boulanger Michel Roux, Sophie returned to London with one mission: to bring the honest, slow craft of French baking to a city that deserved it.

In 2012, with her sister Camille and a single Rofco oven, Stackly opened its doors on Baker Street. The queue formed before 7am on day one, and it has never really stopped. Today we operate two locations, ship nationwide, and employ 24 people — all of whom share the same stubborn belief that shortcuts don't belong in a bakery.

We still use the same starter Sophie fed in 2011. We still open at 7am. We still refuse to compromise.

What We Stand For

Our Philosophy

Six values that guide every decision we make — from ingredient sourcing to how we greet our first customer of the day.

🌾

Farm to Oven

We know the farmers who grow our wheat by name. Short supply chains mean fresher flour, traceability, and a smaller footprint. We visit our suppliers annually.

⏳

Slow is Better

Good bread cannot be rushed. Our sourdoughs ferment for 48 hours, our croissants are laminated over two days, and our fruit tarts are assembled to order.

🙌

Made by Hand

Every item in our cases was shaped, scored, or piped by a human pair of hands. We have one small mixer. We intend to keep it that way.

🌱

Sustainability

All packaging is compostable. Our oven waste heat is recycled. Unsold bread goes to charity. We measure our carbon output and offset the rest.

🎓

Training & Growth

We run apprenticeship programmes with two London colleges and promote from within. Six of our current senior bakers started as weekend assistants.

💛

Community Love

We host monthly baking workshops, donate surplus daily, and sponsor local events. Stackly was built by this neighbourhood — we give back constantly.

Our Journey

Fourteen Years in the Making

2012

A Kitchen and a Dream

Sophie and Camille begin baking sourdough from their Notting Hill flat. Neighbours queue at the door every Sunday morning.

2014

Baker Street Opens

First retail location opens. The queue forms before dawn. The first 200 croissants sell out before 9am — every day for three months.

2016

Best Bakery London

Time Out awards Stackly "Best Artisan Bakery in London." The Great Taste Awards follow with a coveted three-star rating.

2018

Nationwide Delivery Launches

Our online shop goes live. Sourdough and pastries now ship to every corner of the UK, chilled and boxed in compostable packaging.

2020

Custom Cake Studio

We open a dedicated patisserie studio for wedding, celebration and corporate cakes. James Harrington joins as head pastry chef.

2024

Shoreditch Location

Second full bakery opens in Shoreditch. Larger production kitchen enables greater variety and double the daily output.

2026

Celebrating 14 Years

New online home, expanded menu, new faces — same heart. Thank you for every loaf, every morning, every memory shared with us.
